msr-02

## Provenance

- Tool or model: Codex design synthesis
- Prompt: Phone app reset using a soft study desk and page-slip metaphor for CET trust and answer explanation.
- Source context pack: `context-pack.md`
- Artifact: `candidate-proofs/mobile-reset-candidate-proof.html#msr-02`
- Screenshots: `candidate-proofs/mobile-reset-candidate-proof.html#msr-02`

## Product Truth Fit

Preserves CET trust and card-as-object semantics. It can make analysis feel credible. The risk is material density: too much desk/page framing can recreate stacked-card fatigue.

## Focal Object

A study page card sitting on a desk plane.

## First-Read Path

Study page -> margin action -> answer page corner -> quiet navigation.

## Interaction Silhouette

Learning card silhouette with a page-turn detail slip. The silhouette is recognizably a card, not a dashboard.

## Spatial Model

Space appears as a light bookmark/address mark attached to the page. Library / group / box / card remain visible but not dominant.

## State Language

Correctness is shown by a calm page marker. Favorite uses a bookmark tag. Sleep/wake is not foregrounded in this candidate.

## Motion Causality

The answer explanation folds out from the page corner after resolution. This motion is caused by revealing the card back or result, not decoration.

## Platform Strategy

Phone can use a single page and corner slip. Tablet can show the desk margin. PC web can show the page plus side address, but the material metaphor must stay restrained.

## Implementation Mapping

Maps to Learning object plane and answer slip. Future implementation should borrow material continuity but avoid adding many separate panels.

## Known Risks

It may look like a reading app rather than a CET operation app. If the desk metaphor becomes dominant, Space continuity weakens.
