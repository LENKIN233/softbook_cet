## Product Truth

Failure records prevent future agents from reintroducing superficially attractive directions that violate the single-card flow, physical Space hierarchy, or implementation-authority boundary.

## Rejected Patterns

### Timeline-First Study Management

Rejected source: `msr-05`.

Reason: the timeline becomes the app's focal object. This makes the learner manage progress steps rather than operate the current card. It risks turning the product into a study planner.

### Dashboard-First Overview

Rejected source: `msr-06`.

Reason: metrics become the product center. This conflicts with product-core's warning against distracting the user with statistics and system state.

### Carousel-First Space Browsing

Rejected source: `msr-07`.

Reason: browsing becomes the first action. Space hierarchy is visible, but Learning loses primacy and phone containment becomes fragile.

### Result-Report-First Detail

Rejected source: `msr-08`.

Reason: answer detail becomes a report page. The app keeps the current flaw where Learning and Detail feel like separate surfaces rather than two states of one study object.

## Reuse Rule

These patterns can be used as negative examples in future reviews. They should not be used as accepted design authority unless a later search run proves a materially different version that preserves the current-card object, physical Space hierarchy, and one-screen operation.
